Key statutory framework

Family Code section 3044 - Domestic Violence Presumption

Cal. Fam. Code section 3044
Creates a rebuttable presumption that granting custody to a person who has perpetrated domestic violence is detrimental to the best interest of the child. Petitioner bears the burden to rebut it.
Your TDVRO and documented physical intimidation (arm grabbing, intimidating exchanges) directly invoke this presumption.

Family Code section 3020 - Legislative Policy

Cal. Fam. Code section 3020
Declares California public policy to assure children frequent and continuing contact with both parents - except where contact would not be in the child best interest.
Ethan estrangement from Respondent directly conflicts with this policy. Dr. Kelley testimony that he was intimidated and saw Respondent as the primary parent is central.
